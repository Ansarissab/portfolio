  1. 01

    Own product · Open source

    ShopFlare

    A self-hosted alternative to Shopify Basic: storefront, admin, Stripe checkout, cash-on-delivery and WhatsApp ordering, installable as a PWA. It runs on Cloudflare's free tier, so the hosting bill is nothing.

    2,084

    unit tests at 95% coverage, plus integration tests against real workerd and full end-to-end, accessibility and visual-regression suites. 22 architecture decision records. 657 commits.

    Role: sole author, architecture through deploy.

  2. 02

    Multi-tenant SaaS · Private

    Gymplex

    Independent gyms need software, but each one wants its own subdomain, its own branding, and its own money. Gymplex is one Rails monolith serving all of them. Members book classes and pay, staff run check-ins and schedules, and the platform operator administers every gym from one place.

    One codebase, isolated tenants

    Tenant resolution happens in Rack middleware before the router: the subdomain maps to a gym, and every gym-owned model carries a default_scope bound to it. Cross-tenant leakage takes a deliberate, documented bypass, not an accident.

    Role: sole author: product, architecture, build.
    Notable: Stripe Connect with one Standard account per gym. Owners connect their own Stripe by OAuth and get paid directly; the platform takes a configurable application fee. Exactly three scope bypasses exist, each written down and defended: the platform operator, Stripe webhooks resolving tenant from event.account, and background jobs restoring tenant from a job argument.
    Scale: 303 commits over 5.5 weeks, 35 models, 28 migrations, 428 tests.

    • Rails 8.1
    • Ruby 4.0
    • PostgreSQL
    • Stripe Connect
    • Hotwire
    • Slim
    • Tailwind
    • Solid Queue
    • Minitest

06

Live, in this page

Try it rather than take my word for it.

This is my image-to-PDF tool, running right here. Everything happens in your browser. no upload, no server, no account. Open the network tab and check.

Drop images here, or choose files. They never leave this device.

    Why this matters commercially

    Most image-to-PDF sites upload your files to someone else's server. For anyone handling contracts, invoices, medical records or IDs, that is the whole problem. Doing the work client-side removes the liability instead of promising to look after it, and it costs nothing to run, because there is nothing to run.

    How it works

    pdf-lib assembles the document in memory from a FileReader stream, embedding each image and adding a page sized to it. The blob is handed straight to a download. No fetch call exists in this code path.
